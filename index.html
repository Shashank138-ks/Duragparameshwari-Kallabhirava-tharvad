Our Sacred Heritage

History & Significance

The Sri Dhurgaparameshwari Kalabhirava Temple at Kattekal, Kasargod, is a revered seat of divine power nestled in the lush coastal heartland of northern Kerala. Dedicated to the fierce yet compassionate Goddess Dhurgaparameshwari and Lord Kalabhirava, the temple has been a beacon of devotion for countless generations.

Goddess Dhurgaparameshwari — a form of Shakti widely worshipped across the Tulu Nadu and northern Kerala region — is believed to protect devotees from evil, bestow strength, and fulfil sincere prayers. Lord Kalabhirava, the guardian aspect of Shiva, presides over time, justice, and liberation.

The temple follows the Kerala–Tulu Nadu traditions of worship, blending the rich spiritual heritage of both cultures in its rituals, festivals, and customs. Pilgrims from across Kerala, Karnataka, and beyond travel to Kattekal to seek the blessings of the presiding deities.

Noon Pooja (Uchha)

🌤
Sayankala Pooja
5:00 PM
Evening pooja; temple reopens for darshan after afternoon break.
🌙
Athazhapuja (Night)
7:30 PM
Night pooja with deepa aradhana. Temple closes after Doopa Aradhana.

Sacred Calendar

Festivals & Events

March / April
Sri Dhurgaparameshwari Jatre
The grand annual festival of the Goddess with procession, cultural performances and special seva.
October
Navaratri Mahotsava
Nine nights of Devi worship with special Chandi Homa, bhajans and Kumari Puja.
January
Makara Vilakku
Sacred lamp lighting on Makara Sankranti day with special darshan and prasad distribution.
August
Kalabhirava Shashti
Dedicated to Lord Kalabhirava with special abhisheka, Rudra parayana and night pooja.
Monthly
Ashtami & Pradosha
Special poojas on Ashtami (eighth lunar day) and Pradosha (13th lunar day) every month.
Every Friday
Devi Archana
Weekly special archana to Goddess Dhurgaparameshwari with kumkum and flowers.
